PDF vs JPEG: Understanding Your Options

PDF (Portable Document Format) is the gold standard for document sharing, preserving formatting across all devices. However, PDFs can be difficult to edit or display on platforms that don't support them natively.

JPEG (Joint Photographic Experts Group) is the most widely used image format globally. It offers excellent compression with adjustable quality, making it perfect for photographs, web graphics, and social media. JPEG files can be viewed anywhere, edited in any photo software, and easily shared.

Converting from PDF to JPEG gives you the best of both worlds - the structured layout of your original document with the universal accessibility of an image format.
