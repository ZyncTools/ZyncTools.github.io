PNG vs PDF: Understanding Your Options

PNG (Portable Network Graphics) is a raster graphics format known for lossless compression and transparency support. It's ideal for web graphics, logos, and images requiring sharp edges and transparency. However, PNG files can be large and aren't ideal for multi-page documents.

PDF (Portable Document Format) is the universal standard for document sharing. PDFs can contain multiple pages, support vector graphics, and maintain formatting across all devices. They're perfect for presentations, portfolios, and document archiving.

Converting PNG to PDF gives you the best of both worlds - the visual quality of your original images with the professional document format of PDF.
